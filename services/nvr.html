Overview

A Security Camera System That Works When You Need It To.

Consumer-grade cameras and DIY NVR setups fail in predictable ways — poor image quality in low light, storage that fills up and stops recording, systems that go offline and no one notices. When an incident happens, the footage is either missing or unusable.

We design and install commercial-grade IP camera systems built around reliable hardware and proper network infrastructure. Every installation is configured for continuous recording, remote access, and long-term storage — and integrated into your existing network so management is centralized.

Because we handle your network and IT infrastructure, your surveillance system is not a separate, disconnected piece. It is part of a cohesive environment we monitor and maintain alongside everything else.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions.

How long is footage stored?
Storage duration depends on the number of cameras, recording resolution, and motion activity at your location. We size your storage based on your specific retention requirements — typically 30, 60, or 90 days. We can also configure tiered storage to keep recent footage at full resolution and older footage at reduced resolution to extend retention.
Can we access footage remotely?
Yes. We configure secure remote access so you can view live feeds and review recorded footage from a smartphone, tablet, or computer. Access is protected with proper authentication so only authorized users can view your cameras.
Do you work with existing cameras?
It depends on what you have. Many consumer-grade systems are not compatible with commercial NVR infrastructure. We will assess your existing hardware and give you an honest recommendation — reuse what makes sense, replace what does not.
Is this available as a standalone service or does it require managed IT?
NVR installation is available as a standalone project. Ongoing monitoring and support is included if you are on a managed IT plan. Standalone clients can also purchase a separate support agreement for the surveillance system.
