Overview

A Backup You Have Never Tested Is Not a Backup.

Most businesses have some form of backup in place. Very few have actually tested whether it works. When a ransomware attack, hardware failure, or accidental deletion forces a restore, is not the time to find out your backup has been failing silently for six months.

We design and manage backup and disaster recovery solutions powered by Druva — a platform built for reliable, automated, and verifiable data protection across endpoints, servers, and cloud environments.

We back up your data. We verify the backups are running successfully. We test the restore process. And we document a recovery plan so that if something goes wrong, we are not figuring it out from scratch under pressure.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions.

How often is our data backed up?
It depends on your plan and the criticality of the data. Endpoints typically back up continuously or daily. Servers can be backed up as frequently as every 15 minutes for critical workloads. We set retention and frequency based on your business requirements.
Where is backup data stored?
Backup data is stored in secure, encrypted cloud storage through Druva's infrastructure. We can also configure local backup targets for faster on-site recovery when needed.
How long does it take to restore from a backup?
Recovery time depends on the amount of data and the type of restore. Individual file restores can happen in minutes. Full system restores take longer but are executed against a documented plan so the process is as fast as possible.
Does Microsoft 365 back up our data automatically?
No. Microsoft maintains the uptime and availability of their platform, but they do not protect against accidental deletion, malicious activity, or data loss caused by third-party apps. You need a separate backup solution for your Microsoft 365 data.
